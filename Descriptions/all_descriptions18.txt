 Campylobacter californiensis (ca.li.for.ni.ensis. N.L. masc. adj. californiensis, pertaining to California, USA, where these strains were isolated).
Cells are motile. After 72 h culture at 37 C under microaerobic conditions on ABA-blood, colonies are glistening, opaque, convex, and circular with entire margins and are 12 mm in diameter. Cells are slightly curved rods and coccoid cells are observed in some cultures. Growth occurs on ABA-blood at both 37 and 42 C under microaerobic conditions and at 37 C under anaerobic conditions. No growth on ABA-blood at 30 C under microaerobic conditions or at any temperature under aerobic conditions. All strains demonstrate oxidase activity but no catalase, alkaline phosphatase, hippuricase, urease or -haemolytic activity. Does not hydrolyse indoxyl acetate. All strains reduce nitrate and selenite but not triphenyltetrazolium chloride. Growth is supported on ABA-blood with a final NaCl concentration of 2% (w/v) and on ABA-blood supplemented with 1% (w/v) glycine. Most strains grow on ABA-blood supplemented with 0.04% TTC. H2S production on TSI agar is variable. Strains are resistant to 30 mg l1 cephalothin and 30 mg l1 nalidixic acid. Five strains were isolated from cattle and four strains were isolated from feral swine. The type strain, RM6914T (=LMG 32304T=CCUG 75329T), was recovered in 2008 from cow faeces in California. Accession numbers for the 16S rRNA gene and genome sequence of the type strain are MW131429 and CP012545, respectively.

 Rhodobacter flavimaris (fla.vi.maris. L. masc. adj. flavus, yellow; L. neut. n. mare, sea; N.L. gen. n. flavimaris, of the Yellow Sea).
The cells are Gram-stain-negative, non-motile, aerobic, ovoid to short rods, 0.61.0 m wide and 0.82.6 m long. Colonies on R2A agar medium supplemented with 2.5% sea salt (w/v) are circular, smooth, slightly convex and pale yellow in colour (diameter 0.40.5 m). Positive for catalase activity, but negative for oxidase activity. Growth occurs at 2037 C (optimum, 28 C), pH 6.09.0 (optimum, pH 7.08.0), and with 05.0% sodium chloride (optimum, 03.0%). Utilizes bromo-succinic acid, d-arabitol, d-aspartic acid, cellobiose, d-fructose, d-fucose, d-galactose, d-galacturonic acid, d-gluconic acid, d-glucose-6-PO4, d-lactic acid methyl ester, d-malic acid, maltose, d-mannitol, d-mannose, melibiose, raffinose, d-saccharic acid, d-salicin, d-sorbitol, trehalose, turanose, glycerol, glycyl-l-proline, inosine, l-alanine, l-aspartic acid, l-butyric acid, l-fucose, l-galactonic acid lactone, l-glutamic acid, l-lactic acid, l-malic acid, l-pyroglutamic acid, l-rhamnose, l-serine, methyl pyruvate, myo-inositol, N-acetyl-d-galactosamine, N-acetyl--d-mannosamine, N-acetyl neuraminic acid, pectin, propionic acid, quinic acid, stachyose, sucrose, -d-glucose, lactose, -keto-glutaric acid, -hydroxy-d, methyl -d-glucoside, -amino-butyric acid and 3-methyl glucose in Biolog GEN III microtest system. The major isoprenoid quinone is Q-10 and the major polar lipids are DPG, PG, PC, PE, GL, and PME. The DNA G+C content is 64.0 mol%. The major fatty acid is summed features 8 (C18:1 6c and/or C18:1 7c).
The type strain, WL0062T (=MCCC 1K06014T=JCM 34676T=GDMCC 1.2427T), was isolated from marine sediment of the Yellow Sea, PR China. The GenBank/EMBL/DDBJ accession numbers are JAJUOS010000024.1 (16S rRNA gene) and JAJUOS010000000 (genome), respectively.

 Halostreptopolyspora (Ha. lo. strep. to. po. ly. spo'ra. Gr. masc. n. hals, salt; Gr. masc. adj. streptos, pliant, bent; Gr. masc. adj. polys, many; Gr. fem. n. spora, a seed; N. L. fem. n. Halostreptopolyspora halophilic bacteria that produce pliant or bent spore chains.).
Gram-positive-staining, aerobic and moderately halophilic. The substrate mycelia are well developed and non-fragmented. The mature aerial hyphae have single-, double- or triple-wrinkled spore(s). Whole-cell hydrolysates contain meso-DAP and mannose as diagnostic markers. The menaquinones are MK-10(H8), MK-10(H6) and MK-9(H10). The polar lipids are diphosphatidylglycerol, phosphatidylglycerol, phosphatidylcholine, unidentified phospholipids and unidentified glycolipid. The major fatty acids are iso-C15:0 and anteiso-C15:0. The type spcies is Halostreptopolyspora alba.
 Description of Halostreptopolyspora gen. nov.Description of Halostreptopolyspora alba sp. nov.
 Halostreptopolyspora alba (alba. L. fem. adj. alba, white).
Displays the following properties in addition to those given in the genus description. Well-formed aerial mycelium when cultured on ISP 2 and nutrient agar. Moderate growth on ISP 3 and Cazpeks agar, weak growth on ISP 4 and ISP 5 modified with the addition of 10% (w/v) NaCl and adjusting pH to 7.5 with 10 M NaOH. No growth on potatodextrose agar medium. Colony colour is dependent on the test medium. Substrate mycelium colour is white on ISP 3, 4 and 5, Czapeks agar and nutrient agar, while no discernible colour on ISP 2. Aerial mycelium is white on ISP 3, 4 and 5 and Czapeks agar. Diffusible pigment is not observed on any media tested. Growth occurs at pH 6.09.0, with 328 % (w/v) NaCl, and at 1045 C; optimal growth occurs at pH 7.08.0, with 516% (w/v) NaCl and at 37 C. Cellobiose, l-malic acid, myo-inositol, d-ribose, l-arabinose, lactose, creatinine, raffinose, l-rhamnose, cyclodextrin, citric acid, d-xylose, d-sorbitol, sucrose, d-galactose, salicin, d-arabinose, d-mannitol and xylitol can be used as sole carbon sources, but not dextrin, glycerin, trehalose and -d-glucose. Hypoxanthine, leucine, threonine, proline, methionine, histidine, glutamine and cysteine can be utilized as sole nitrogen sources, but not leucine, isoleucine, adenine and l-asparagine. Positive for catalase activity, oxidase activity, gelatin liquefaction, nitrate reduction and hydrolysis of Tweens 20, 40, 60 and 80. Major cellular fatty acids are anteiso-C15:0 and iso-C15:0. Susceptible to (mg per disc): tetracycline (30), erythromycin (15), kanamycin (30), ciprofloxacin (5), vancomycin (30), clindamycin (2), clarithromycin (15), furazolidone (300), trimethoprim (5), neomycin (30) and midecamycin (30). Resistant to (mg per disc): oxacillin (1), gentamicin (10), polymyxin (300), ofloxacin (5), streptomycin (10), tobramycin (10), amoxicillin (20), penicillin (10), amphotericin (30), ampicillin (10) and netilmicin (30). Results from API 50CH tests show that acids are produced from glycerol, d-glucose, d-mannose, inositol, N-acetylglucosamine, arbutin, aesculin, salicin, cellobiose, maltose and d-tagatose. In the API ZYM test, positive for alkaline phosphatase, esterase C4, esterase lipase C8, leucine arylamidase, valine arylamidase, cystine arylamidase, -chymotrypsin, acid phosphatase, naphthol-AS-BI-phosphohydrolase, -galactosidase, -glucuronidase, -glucosidase, -glucosidase and N-acetyl--glucosaminidase. The whole-cell sugars are glucose, galactose and rhamnose.
The type strain, YIM 96095T (=KCTC 49266T=CGMCC 4.7636T), was isolated from a soil sample collected from Aiding Lake, Xinjiang province, north-west China. The DNA G+C content of the type strain is 69.7 mol%. The NCBI GenBank accession number for the 16S rRNA gene sequence of strain YIM 96095T is MK182318. The draft whole genome sequence for YIM 96095T has been deposited at DDBJ/ENA/GenBank under accession number GCA_003725585.1.
