Description of Symmetrospora pini P. Wang & F.L. HUI sp. nov.
 Symmetrospora pini (pini. N.L. gen. n. pini, referring to Pinus, the plant genus from which the type strain was isolated).
On YM agar after 7days at 20C, the streak culture is salmon pink to red, butyrous, smooth and semi-glossy with an entire margin (Fig. 2a). In YM broth after 3days at 20C, cells are ellipsoidal to cylindrical (3.54.469.1m) and occur singly. Budding is lateral (Fig. 2b). After 1month at 20C in stationary cultures, a sediment and a ring are present. In Dalmau plate culture on CM agar, pseudohyphae and hyphae were not formed. Sexual structures are not observed in any of the strains or when strains are paired on the tested media after 6weeks at 17C. Ballistoconidia are not produced on CM agar. Glucose fermentation is absent. Glucose, lactose (delayed and weak), cellobiose (delayed and weak), d-xylose (delayed and weak), d-arabinose (weak), glycerol (delayed and weak), d-mannitol (delayed and weak), d-glucosamine (delayed and weak), d-glucuronate (delayed and weak) and glucono-1,5-lactone are assimilated as sole carbon sources. Inulin, sucrose, raffinose, melibiose, galactose, trehalose, maltose, melezitose, methyl--d-glucoside, salicin, l-sorbose, l-rhamnose, l-arabinose, 5-keto-d-gluconate, d-ribose, methanol, ethanol, erythritol, ribitol, galactitol, d-glucitol, myo-inositol, dl-lactate, succinate, citrate, d-gluconate, N-acetyl-d-glucosamine and 2-keto-d-gluconate are not assimilated. Nitrate (delayed and weak) and nitrite (delayed and weak) are assimilated as sole nitrogen sources. Ethylamine, l-lysine and cadaverine are not assimilated. Maximum growth temperature is 30C. Growth in vitamin-free medium is negative. Growth on 50% (w/w) glucose-yeast extract agar is negative. Starch-like substances are not produced. Hydrolysis of urea and Diazonium Blue B reaction are positive.
Holotype CICC 33601T was isolated from a leaf of P. massoniana collected in Sifangjing Village, Pingtang County, Guizhou Province, China, in February 2023. It has been preserved in a metabolically inactive state in the China Centre of Industrial Culture Collection (CICC), Beijing, PR China. The ex-type culture has been deposited in a metabolically inactive state in the Portuguese Yeast Culture Collection (PYCC), Caparica, Portugal, as strain PYCC 9982 (=NYNU 232201). The GenBank/EMBL/DDBJ/PIR accession numbers for the sequences of the D1/D2 domain of the LSU rRNA gene and the ITS region determined in this study are OQ851926 and OQ851927 for CICC 33601T and OQ851928 and OQ857286 for NYNU 232218, respectively. The MycoBank number is MB 857729.

 Fonsecazyma yulaniae (yu.la'ni.ae. N.L. gen. n. yulaniae, of the plant species Y. denudata, from which flowers it has been isolated).
The cells are ovoid to ellipsoid (2.15.42.85.9m) and occur singly or in pairs after 3days of growth in YM broth at 25 C. Multilateral budding is observed (Fig. 2a). After growth in YM broth for 1month at 25C, a ring and a sediment are formed. The streak culture is pale yellow-cream, glistening, butyrous and smooth with an entire margin after 1month of incubation on YM agar at 25 C. No hyphae or pseudohyphae are observed on CMA, potato dextrose agar (PDA) and YM agar. Additionally, sexual reproduction is not observed on CMA, MEA, Fowells acetate agar or PDA agar under the same conditions. The strain could grow (weak) at 30C but could not grow at 35C. Growth in YM broth with 50% (w/v) glucose and in the vitamin-free medium is positive. Growth in YM broth with 10% (w/v) sodium chloride is negative. Additionally, the strain shows positive urease activity and a positive diazonium blue B reaction. Fermentation of glucose, galactose, sucrose, maltose, lactose and raffinose was absent. Glucose, galactose, sucrose, maltose, cellobiose, trehalose, raffinose, melezitose, inulin, soluble starch, d-xylose, d-ribose, l-arabinose, l-rhamnose, ethanol, glycerol (weak), ribitol, galactitol, d-mannitol, d-glucitol, methyl--d-glucoside, salicin (weak), succinic acid and inositol are assimilated as sole carbon sources. l-Sorbose, lactose, melibiose, methanol, erythritol, dl-lactate and citrate are not assimilated. Ethylamine hydrochloride, cadaverine, l-lysine, creatine (weak) and creatinine are assimilated as sole nitrogen sources, whereas nitrate and nitrite are not assimilated.
The holotype, CGMCC 2.5852T, was isolated from the flowers of Y. denudata collected from the Beijing Olympic Forest Park, PR China. It has been deposited in a metabolically inactive state in the CGMCC, Beijing, PR China. The ex-type culture has been deposited in the Biological Resource Center, NITE (NBRC), Japan, as NBRC 114206. The GenBank/EMBL/DDBJ accession number for the 26S rRNA gene D1/D2 domain and the ITS sequence of strain CGMCC 2.5852T are PQ811590 and PQ805397. Its taxonomic description has been registered in Fungal Names (FN572294).
 Description of Fonsecazyma yulaniae Y.J. Liao, X. Zhang and A.H. Li sp. nov.Description of Planosporangium spinosum sp. nov.
 Planosporangium spinosum (spi.nosum. L. neut. adj. spinosum, spiny, referring to the spiny spore surface).
Gram-stain-positive, aerobic, non-motile, catalase-positive, oxidase-negative and spore-forming actinobacterium. Globose spores with a spiny surface are produced on a short sporophore of the substrate mycelium. The colony colour ranged from vivid orangeyellow to yellow. Diffusible pigment and melanin production are absent in all tested media. The strain grows well on ISP2, ISP3, ISP4, ISP7, MBA, GYE, YSA and CZA media and moderately on ISP5, PDA and NA, with optimal growth at temperatures between 35 and 41C and at pH 67. The strain can tolerate 3% NaCl. Nitrate reduction and hydrogen sulphide production are negative. Hydrolysis of aesculin, arbutin, starch, Tween 20 and Tween 80 is positive, while hydrolysis of CM-cellulose, gelatin, guanine, hypoxanthine, skimmed milk, l-tyrosine, urea, xanthine and xylan is negative. Strain 12N6T utilizes dextrose, l-arabinose, cellobiose, d-fructose, d-galactose, d-glucose, glycerol, maltose, d-mannose, lactose, raffinose, sodium acetate, sodium propionate, sucrose, trehalose and d-xylose as sole carbon sources but does not utilize myo-inositol, d-mannitol, l-rhamnose, d-ribose, d-sorbitol and xylitol. l-Asparagine, glycine and l-histidine are utilized as sole nitrogen sources, while hydroxyproline, KNO3 and methionine are not. The whole-cell hydrolysates contain meso-diaminopimelic acid, and the whole-cell sugars are glucose, mannose, arabinose, xylose and ribose. The polar lipid profile consists of DPG, PE, PG and two unidentified phospholipids. The major cellular fatty acids are anteiso-C17:0, iso-C16:0 and iso-C15:0. The predominant MKs are MK-9(H4), MK-9(H6), MK-9(H8) and MK-10(H4). Mycolic acid is not detected in the cells.
The type strain is 12N6T=TBRC 19149T=NBRC 117121T, which was isolated from soils of peat swamp forest collected from Rayong Botanical Garden in Rayong Province, Thailand. The DNA G+C content is 71.6 mol%. The GenBank genome accession number and the GenBank of 16S rRNA gene sequence of strain 12N6T are JBLYUB01 and PV175756, respectively.

 Dickeya ananatis (a.na.natis. N.L. masc. n. Ananas, generic name of the pineapple; N.L. gen. masc. n. ananatis, of Ananas, of the pineapple).
D. ananatis is a Gram-negative, rod-shaped, motile bacterium measuring 2.910.02m in length, with peritrichous flagella. It is fermentative and grows optimally at 28C on nutrient agar medium supplemented with 0.4% glucose and on Kings B medium, forming milky white colonies within 24h. This bacterium forms pits on the CVP medium within 2448h [33] and causes maceration of potato tubers, taro slices and pineapple leaves. D. ananatis sp. nov. is catalase positive, oxidase negative, pectinolytic and positive for nitrate reduction. D. ananatis sp. nov. is able to utilize sucrose, d-raffinose, d-melibiose, -methyl-d-glucoside, d-salicin, N-acetyl-d-glucosamine, d-glucose, d-mannose, d-fructose, d-galactose, d-sorbitol, d-mannitol, myo-inositol, glycerol, d-glucose-6-PO4, d-fructose-6-PO4, d-aspartic acid, l-aspartic acid, l-glutamic acid, l-serine, pectin, d-galacturonic acid, l-galactonic acid lactone, d-gluconic acid, d-glucuronic acid, methyl pyruvate, l-lactic acid, citric acid, d-malic acid, l-malic acid, bromo-succinic acid, acetic acid and formic acid as carbon sources. It shows inhibition at pH 5, or in the presence of 8% NaCl, d-serine, minocycline, nalidixic acid, potassium tellurite, aztreonam and sodium bromate. The type strain is unable to utilize the following carbon sources: dextrin, stachyose, N-acetyl neuraminic acid, d-serine, quinic acid, p-hydroxy-phenylacetic acid, d-lactic acid methyl ester, Tween 40, -amino-butyric acid, -hydroxy-butyric acid, -hydroxy-D, l-butyric acid, -keto-butyric acid and propionic acid. Enzymatic reactions tested with the API ZYM kit give positive results for alkaline phosphatase, esterase, leucine arylamidase, acid phosphatase, naphthol-AS-BI-phosphohydrolase, -galactosidase and -glucosidase. The type strain is able to utilize citrate, produces indole and acetoin and is able to hydrolyse aesculin; it is negative for urease. Assays for antibiotic susceptibility showed that D. ananatis sp. nov. A5410T is sensitive to 13 antibiotics including trimethoprim, kanamycin, bacitracin, cephalexin, hygromycin B, vancomycin, cefotaxime, tetracycline, spectinomycin, chloramphenicol, carbenicillin, penicillin and gentamicin. The highest sensitivity was observed for cefotaxime, carbenicillin and cefalexins. The D. ananatis sp. nov. type strain is A5410T (=ICMP 25020T=LMG 33197T) isolated from pineapple in Hawaii. Its genome size is 4.77 Mbp with a G+C content of 53.5mol% based on the genome sequence. Other isolates of this species were also obtained from pineapple in Hawaii (A5391, A6136, A6137 and A5611), and two strains, CFBP 1272 and CFBP 1278, were isolated from pineapple in Malaysia.
The 16S rRNA gene sequence is available in GenBank (OR271567). The type strain A5410T genome from a previous study can be found under CP040816 (BioProject PRJNA544764, BioSample SAMN11855518). The genomes sequenced in this study have been submitted to the database with the following accession numbers: CP040816, CP146223, CP146227, CP146228, CP146224, CP146225 and CP146226.

 Chryseobacterium gossypii (gos.sypi.i. N.L. gen. n. gossypii. of Gossypium, referring to the isolation of the type strain from cotton field).
The cells are aerobic, Gram-negative, non-motile and do not form spores. They test positive for catalase and oxidase. The bacteria appear as straight rods and form yellow colonies with smooth edges on R2A agar. They grow at temperatures ranging from 20 to 45C, with an optimal temperature at 37C. The pH range for growth is between 5.0 and 10.0, with an optimum between 6.0 and 9.0. The cells can proliferate in the presence of 0%10% NaCl, with the optimal salt concentration for growth conditions being 1%. The following reactions are positive: nitrate reduction; glucose fermentation; hydrolysis of aesculin, starch, gelatin and p-nitrophenyl--d-galactopyranoside; fermentation of glucose, arabinose, mannose, N-acetyl-glucosamine, maltose and gluconate. The following reactions are negative: indole production; hydrolysis of arginine and urea; assimilation of mannitol, caprylic acid, hexanedioic acid, malic acid, citric acid and phenylacetic acid. Positive enzyme activities include alkaline phosphatase, esterase, leucine arylamidase, naphthol-AS-BI-phosphohydrolase and -glucosidase. Trace enzyme activities include lipase, -galactosidase and -glucosidase. The enzyme activities of lipase, valine arylamidase, cysteine arylamidase, trypsin, chymotrypsin, acid phosphatase, -galactosidase, -glucuronidase, N-acetyl-glucosaminidase, -mannosidase and -fucosidase are negative. The major cellular fatty acids are iso-C15:0, iso-C17:0 3OH, summed feature 3 and summed feature 9. Strain 1RM2T contains MK-6 as the respiratory quinone. The principal polar lipids include phosphatidylethanolamine, unidentified aminophospholipids and unidentified lipids.
The strain type is 1RM2T (=GDMCC 1.4437T=KCTC 102275T), isolated from continuous cotton field soil samples in Xinjiang. The DNA G+C content is 39.5mol%. The GenBank accession numbers of the 16S rRNA gene and whole-genome sequences of strain 1RM2T are PP663718 and JBGCUR000000000.

 Faucicola osloensis (os.lo.ensis. N.L. fem. adj. osloensis, pertaining to Oslo, Norway, where the species was first recognized).
Basonym: Moraxella osloensis Bvre and Henriksen 1967.
Description: The description follows that of Bvre and Henriksen 1967 (Approved Lists 1980) [41] with some updates [37]. Non-motile, rod-shaped bacterium that is both catalase- and oxidase-positive. It does not produce acids from glucose, hydrolyse Tween 80 or liquefy gelatin. While it lacks haemolytic activity, it exhibits weak DNase activity and can weakly reduce nitrate. It does not produce indole nor display esterase or acid phosphatase activity. The major fatty acids include C18:0 and C18:1 9c. Its genomic G+C content ranges from 43% to 46%.
Type strain: A1920T=ATCC 19976T=CCUG 350T=CIP 68.35T=DSM 6998T=LMG 5131T=NCTC 10465T.

 Description of Faucicola boevrei comb. nov.Description of the genus Lwoffella gen. nov.
 Lwoffella (Lwoff.ella. N.L. fem. dim. n. Lwoffella, named in honour of Andr Lwoff, a pioneering microbiologist whose contributions significantly advanced the taxonomy of Moraxella and bacterial systematics).
Description: Non-motile bacterium with cocci or rod morphology. It is catalase- and oxidase-positive, but does not grow on MacConkey agar or produce acids from glucose. The bacterium lacks growth on minimal medium, haemolysis, proteolysis on Lffler slants and hydrolysis of Tween 80. It does not liquefy gelatin or show alkaline phosphatase activity. Nitrate reduction and DNase activity are present, while indole, phenylalanine deaminase, esterase and acid phosphatase activities are absent. Major fatty acids include C16:1 9 and C18:19. Its genome G+C content is 42.5%.
The type species is Lwoffella lincolnii comb. nov.
