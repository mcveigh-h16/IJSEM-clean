Description of Moraxella veridica sp. nov.
 Moraxella veridica (ve.ridi.ca. L. fem. adj. veridica, truthful).
Cells are Gram-negative cocci that can occur singly, in pairs or in small clumps. Cells are catalase- and oxidase-positive. Cells grow well in BHI agar or broth and in trypticase soy agar or broth at 37C under aerobic conditions. Colonies are entire, smooth, circular and opaque but can form a faint brown pigment when grown on MuellerHinton agar [26] and are tan on BHI agar. On trypticase soy agar with 5% defibrinated sheeps blood under aerobic conditions at 37C, colonies are grey after 24h. Motility was not observed. Cells are positive for leucine arylamidase, phenylalanine arylamidase, tyrosine arylamidase, Ala-Phe-Pro-arylamidase, GGT and Ellmans reagent activity. Cells are negative for arginine arylamidase in contrast to M. catarrhalis. Cells are positive for l-lysine-arylamidase in contrast to M. canis. Cells are also negative for assimilation of galactose, glucose, mannose, maltose and sucrose and negative for urease, ornithine decarboxylase and N-acetyl-d-glucosamine. Major fatty acids are C18:1cis-9 (53.7%), C16:1cis-9 (16.7%) and C12:0 3-OH (6.7%), whilst C17:1 B is absent [26].
The genome of the type strain is 2.2 Mbp, and the DNA G+C content is 43.6mol%.
The type strain was isolated at the Royal Naval Hospital in Chatham, UK, in 1932 and deposited into the American Type Culture Collection as ATCC 23246T and the NCTC as NCTC 4103T.
The genome assembly for ATCC 23246T can be found in the AGP (https://genomes.atcc.org/genomes/6547647b5a0f460d) and in NCBI (CP170124). The 16S23S rRNA IGS region accession is PQ314867, and the combined 16S rRNA gene, 16S23S rRNA IGS region and 23S rRNA gene accession is PQ314868.

 Humidisolicoccus (Hu.mi.di.so.li.coccus. L. masc. adj. humidus, humid; L. neut. n. solum, soil; L. masc. n. coccus (from G. masc. n. kokkos, grain), a coccus; N.L. masc. n. Humidisolicoccus, a coccus inhabiting humid soil).
Gram-stain-positive, aerobic and non-motile cocci. Endospores are not produced. The predominant menaquinones are MK-11, MK-12 and MK-10. The polar lipids contain DPG, PG, PI, and ALs. The cellular fatty acid profile is dominated by anteiso-C15:0, anteiso-C17:0 and iso-C15:0. Phylogenetically, the genus belongs to the family Microbacteriaceae. The type species is Humidisolicoccus flavus.
 Description of Humidisolicoccus gen. nov.Description of Humidisolicoccus flavus sp. nov.
 Humidisolicoccus flavus (flavus. L. masc. adj. flavus, yellow, referring to the colour of the colonies).
Cells are oval-shaped with 0.70.9m in size. Colonies are circular, yellow colour, convex and smooth. Colony diameters are in the range of 24mm. Grows at 2033C (optimum, 30C), pH 7.011 (optimum, 8.0) and in the presence of up to 2% NaCl (optimum, 0%). Starch and esculin are hydrolysed. Positive for esterase (C4), esterase lipase (C8), leucine arylamidase, trypsin, naphthol-AS-BI-phosphohydrolase and -glucosidase, and negative for alkaline phosphatase, lipase (C14), valine arylamidase, cystine arylamidase, -chymotrypsin, acid phosphatase, -galactosidase, -glucuronidase, -glucosidase, N-acetyl--glucosaminidase, -mannosidase and -fucosidase. Casein, Tweens 20, 40 and 80, CMC, DNA, tributyrin, hypoxanthine and tyrosine are not hydrolysed. The genome size of the type strain is 2.5 Mbp, and the G+C content is 62.6%. The type strain is strain MMS21-DH1MA12T (= KCTC 49751T = LMG 32525 T=JCM 37009T), which was isolated from riverside soil.
The GenBank accession no. for the 16S rRNA gene sequence of the type strain is MZ959855, and that of the whole genome sequence is CP142724.

 Vreelandella arctica sp. (arc'ti.ca. L. fem. adj. arctica, referring to the Arctic Ocean, where the type strain was isolated).
Cells are Gram-negative, rod shaped and about 1.82.2m in length and 0.40.6m in width and have flagella. The colonies are round, opaque white, smooth and moist, with regular edges, no halo, a central bulge and a diameter of about 3mm. Growth occurs at 441C (optimum, 28C), pH 611 (optimum, pH 7) and 0.522% (w/v) NaCl (optimum, 68%, w/v). This species has the ability to degrade alkanes and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons and can grow with dodecane, hexadecane, naphthalene, phenanthrene, pyrene and benzo[a]pyrene as the single carbon source. In the API ZYM test, positive for alkaline phosphatase, esterase (C4), esterase lipase (C8) (weak), lipase (C14) (weak), leucine arylamidase (weak), valine arylamidase, cystine arylamidase (weak), acid phosphatase and naphthol-AS-BI-phosphohydrolase. In the API 20NE test, positive for denitrification, d-glucose fermentation, UREase, -glucosidase (aesculin hydrolysis), d-glucose, l-arabinose, d-mannitol, N-acetyl-glucosamine (weak), potassium gluconate, adipic acid and malic acid (weak). In the API 20E test, positive for citrate utilization and fermentation/oxidation of glucose (weak), rhamnose (weak), melibiose and arabinose. Positive for the utilization of dextrin (weak), d-cellobiose (weak), -d-glucose, d-fructose, d-galactose, d-fucose (weak), d-sorbitol, d-mannitol, d-arabitol, glycerol (weak), d-fructose-6-PO4 (weak), l-alanine, l-aspartic acid (weak), l-glutamic acid, d-galacturonic acid (weak), l-galactonic acid lactone (weak), d-gluconic acid, d-glucuronic acid (weak), glucuronamide (weak), l-lactic acid, citric acid, -keto-glutaric acid, D/L-malic acid, bromo-succinic acid (weak), -hydroxybutyric acid (weak), -hydroxy-D, l-butyric acid, acetoacetic acid (weak), propionic acid (weak) and acetic acid. The predominant fatty acids (>10%) are sum in feature 8 (C18:17c and/or C18:16c), C19:0 cyclo 8c, C16:0, C17:0 cyclo and C12:0 3OH. Q-9 is the major respiratory quinone. The predominant polar lipids are DPG, PE and PG.
The type strain R06ZXJ2T (=MCCC 1A08467T=KCTC 92631T) was isolated from the sediments collected in the Arctic Ocean. The DNA G+C content of the type strain is 54.4mol%. The GenBank/EMBL/DDBJ accession numbers for the 16S rRNA gene and the draft genome of strain R06ZXJ2T are OQ363179 and JBANSN000000000, respectively.

 Vreelandella indica sp. (in'di.ca. L. fem. adj. indica, referring to the Indian Ocean, where the type strain was isolated).
Cells are Gram-negative, rod shaped and about 1.92.2m in length and 0.40.6m in width and have flagella and lipids around. The colonies are round, beige and translucent, with a smooth and moist surface, regular edges, no halo, a central bulge and a diameter of about 23mm. Growth occurs at 441C (optimum, 28C), pH 610 (optimum, pH 7) and 0.522% (w/v) NaCl (optimum, 810%, w/v). This species has the ability to degrade alkanes and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons and can grow with dodecane, hexadecane, tetracosane, naphthalene, phenanthrene, pyrene and benzo[a]pyrene as the single carbon source. In the API ZYM test, positive for alkaline phosphatase, esterase (C4), esterase lipase (C8) (weak), lipase (C14) (weak), leucine arylamidase, valine arylamidase, cystine arylamidase (weak) and naphthol-AS-BI-phosphohydrolase (weak). In the API 20NE test, positive for -glucosidase (aesculin hydrolysis), d-glucose, d-mannitol, potassium gluconate and malic acid. Positive for the utilization of dextrin (weak), d-cellobiose (weak), gentiobiose (weak), d-turanose (weak), -d-glucose, d-fructose, d-galactose, 3-methyl glucose (weak), d-fucose (weak), l-fucose (weak), l-rhamnose (weak), d-sorbitol, d-mannitol, d-arabitol, glycerol, d-fructose-6-PO4 (weak), l-alanine, l-aspartic acid, l-glutamic acid, l-serine (weak), d-galacturonic acid (weak), l-galactonic acid lactone (weak), d-gluconic acid, d-glucuronic acid (weak), glucuronamide (weak), d-lactic acid methyl ester (weak), l-lactic acid, citric acid, -keto-glutaric acid, D/L-malic acid, bromo-succinic acid (weak), -hydroxybutyric acid (weak), -hydroxy-D, l-butyric acid, -keto-butyric acid, acetoacetic acid, propionic acid (weak) and acetic acid. The predominant fatty acids (>10%) are C19:0 cyclo 8c, sum in feature 8 (C18:17c and/or C18:16c) and C16:0. Q-9 is the major respiratory quinone. The predominant polar lipids are DPG, PE and PG.
The type strain DY715-9T (=MCCC 1A12469T=KCTC 92628T) was isolated from the TVG surface sediment samples collected in the southwestern Indian Ocean. The DNA G+C content of the type strain is 54.4mol%. The GenBank/EMBL/DDBJ accession numbers for the 16S rRNA gene and the draft genome of strain DY715-9T are OQ363177 and JBANSO000000000, respectively.

 Torulaspora lindneri (lind ne.ri. N.L. gen. n. lindneri, of Paul Lindner, named in honour of his contribution to the establishment of the genus).
Culture characteristics: After the growth on YM agar for 3days at 25C, colonies are white, butyrous, circular, slightly raised, with a smooth surface and entire margins. Cells are circular with a diameter of 2.55m. Budding is multilateral and pseudohyphae or true hyphae are not formed (Fig. 2a). After 1month in YM broth at 25C, sediment is present, but no pellicle is observed. After the growth on V8 agar for 15days at 25C, persistent asci are formed through conjugation between a cell and its bud or between independent cells, each containing one or two spherical ascospores (Fig. 2b).

 Physiological and biochemical characteristics: Sugar fermentation: d-glucose (or weak), sucrose and maltose are positive, while raffinose, d-galactose, lactose and trehalose are negative. Carbon compound assimilation: d-glucose, sucrose (slow), maltose (slow), trehalose (weak), melezitose (or slow), inulin (or delayed) and -methyl-d-glucoside (weak or slow) are positive, while d-galactose, l-sorbose, cellobiose, lactose, melibiose, raffinose (various), soluble starch, d-xylose, l-arabinose, d-arabinose, d-ribose, l-rhamnose, d-glucosamine, methanol, ethanol (or weak), glycerol, erythritol, ribitol, galactitol, d-mannitol (or weak), d-glucitol, salicin, d-glucuronic acid, d-galacturonic acid, dl-lactic acid, succinic acid, sodium citrate dihydrate, inositol, hexadecane, N-acetyl-d-glucosamine and xylitol are negative. Nitrogen compound assimilation: l-lysine (or weak) and ammonium sulphate are positive, while cadaverine dihydrochloride (or weak), potassium nitrate, ethylamine hydrochloride and sodium nitrite are negative. Growth in vitamin-free medium, 10% NaCl plus 5% glucose medium (weak) and on 50% and 60% (w/v) glucose are positive. Diazonium Blue B reaction, urease activity, liquefaction of gelatin and production of extracellular starch-like compounds are negative. The growth on YPD agar at 30C and 37C (weak) is positive, and negative at 40C.
The holotype CGMCC 2.7783 (original number HE144P-2) was isolated from the bark of a chestnut tree collected from Guangshan County, Xinyang, Henan, China, in August 2023, and was deposited in a metabolically inactive state in the China General Microbiological Culture Collection Centre (CGMCC), Beijing, China. An ex-type culture has been deposited in the Japan Collection of Microorganisms (JCM), Koyadai, Japan, as JCM 36914. The GenBank/EMBL/DDBJ accession numbers for the sequences of the D1/D2 domain of strains CGMCC 2.7783 and KKU-PM21 are PP235589 and AB714269, respectively, and of the ITS region are PQ498696 and PQ528005, respectively. The Fungal Names number is FN 572228.
