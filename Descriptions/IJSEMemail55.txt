Description of Pseudidiomarina salilacus sp. nov.
 Pseudidiomarina salilacus sp. nov. (sa.li.lacus. L. masc. n. sal, salt; L. masc. n. lacus, a lake; N.L. gen. masc. n. salilacus, of a salt lake).
Exhibits the following characteristics in addition to those given in the genus description. Cells are Gram-stain-negative, aerobic and non-flagellated with the size of 0.20.6m in width and 1.01.8m in length. Colonies are milky yellow, almost round in shape, transparent, smooth and with a diameter of 27mm. Growth occurs in the temperature range of 1542 (optimum 3237C), at pH 6.010.0 (optimum 7.08.0) and in the presence of up to 11.0% (w/v) NaCl concentrations (optimum 3%, w/v). The strain is positive for the hydrolysis of Tween 80 and starch, but negative for nitrate reduction, urease, H2S production, hydrolysis of Tween 40 or gelatin. The strain YC-516-91T could use benzoate, cellobiose, d-fructose, d-galactose, l-glutamate, maltose, l-ornithine, l-rhamnose, d-sorbitol and sucrose as sole carbon sources; however, acetate, l-arabinose, citrate, formate, d-glucose, glycerol, inositol, lactose, malate, d-mannitol, d-mannose, melibiose, pyruvate, raffinose, d-ribose, succinate and d-xylose are not utilized.
The type strain YC-516-91T (=KCTC 8778T=MCCC 1K09529T) was isolated from a sediment sample collected from Yuncheng Salt Lake in China. The GenBank/EMBL/DDBJ accession numbers for the 16S rRNA gene sequence and draft genome are PQ302160 and JBKIWV000000000, respectively.
GenBank accession numbers for the 16S rRNA gene are PQ302159 (YC-516-48) and PQ302161 (YC-516-93). The Whole Genome Shotgun projects have been deposited at DDBJ/ENA/GenBank under the accessions JBKIWU000000000 (YC-516-48) and JBKIWW000000000 (YC-516-93).

 Metschnikowia ratanii (ra.tani.i. N.L. gen. n. ratanii is named in honour of Sir Ratan Tata, an Indian industrialist and philanthropist, for his charitable contributions, establishment of various institutes in India and funding of numerous research programmes worldwide).

 Fibrella aquatica sp. nov. (a.qua'ti.ca. L. fem. adj. aquatica, living in water).
Cells are Gram-negative, non-motile, rod-shaped and facultatively anaerobic. They measure approximately 2.64.0m in length and 0.60.9m in width. After cultivation on R2A agar at 30C for 4days, the strain forms convex, circular colonies with smooth, entire edges and reddish pigmentation. The cells exhibit robust growth on R2A agar but weak growth on NA. The strain can grow at temperatures ranging from 10 C to 35 C and at pH levels ranging from 6.0 to 12.0 and can tolerate up to 0.5% NaCl. Optimal growth occurs at a temperature of 25C, at a pH level of 7.0 and in the absence of NaCl. Catalase and oxidase reactions are positive. The cells can hydrolyze CM-cellulose but not starch, chitin, casein, Tween 20, Tween 80 and DNase. In the API 20NE test, the strain tests positive for aesculin and gelatin hydrolysis, as well as -galactosidase activity; however, it tests negative for nitrate reduction, indole production, glucose fermentation, arginine dihydrolase and urease. It also tests negative for the assimilation of d-glucose, l-arabinose, d-mannose, d-mannitol, N-acetyl-glucosamine, d-maltose, potassium gluconate, capric acid, adipic acid, malic acid, trisodium citrate and phenylacetic acid. The predominant cellular fatty acids (>10%) are summed feature 3 (including C16:16c and/or C16:17c), C16:0 and C16:15c. MK-7 is the major respiratory quinone. The polar lipids include PE, two unidentified lipids, four unidentified ALs, five unidentified GLs, three unidentified APLs and one unidentified APGL.
Strain GW2-5T (=KACC 23817T=TBRC 19128T) was isolated from a freshwater sample collected from Gimpo-si, Gyeonggi-do, Republic of Korea. The genomic DNA G+C content of the type strain is 51.5 mol%. The GenBank/EMBL/DDBJ/PIR accession numbers of the whole-genome shotgun and 16S rRNA gene sequences are JBGGWF000000000 and PP976588, respectively.

Isolates produce a diffusible fluorescent pigment when grown on Kings medium B; are negative for levan production, oxidase activity, pectolytic activity on potato and arginine dihydrolase activity; and do not elicit an HR in either tobacco or tomato plants. Isolates were not observed to cause disease when spray-inoculated on cantaloupe (Cucumis melo), watermelon (Citrullus lanatus) and squash (Cucurbita pepo subsp. pepo) seedlings. Isolates were observed to grow at pH levels of 5 and 6 as well as at salinity levels of 1%, 4% and 8% NaCl. Growth of isolate 22-Al-Cl-001T suspended in tryptic soy broth (Sigma-Aldrich) was assessed at incubation temperatures of 0, 4, 15, 21, 28, 32, 37 and 41 C. After 48 h of incubation, growth was observed only in cultures incubated at temperatures between 15 and 37 C, with optimal growth occurring between 21 and 32 C. Type strain 22-AL-CL-001T (NCPPB 4760=LMG 33363) was isolated from cantaloupe foliage displaying marginal leaf necrosis and blighting in Alabama, USA. NCBI accession numbers for P. alabamensis genomes are as follows: 22-AL-Cl-001T, GCA_030580815.1; USDA-ARS-USMARC-56711, GCA_001534745.1. Cells are Gram-stain-negative, non-spore-forming, aerobic, motile and rod-shaped (0.40.6m in width and 1.31.8m in length). Growth occurs in the presence of NaCl concentrations ranging from 0.5 to 7% (w/v) with an optimum at 3%, a pH range of 5.58.5 (optimum at 7.0) and a temperature of 3060 C (optimum at 50 C). Catalase-positive and oxidase-negative. Positive for hydrolysis of aesculin, gelatin and PNPG. Negative for nitrate reduction, indole production, fermentation of d-glucose, arginine dihydrolase and urease. All assimilation results for API 20NE are negative.  Corynebacterium rhinophilum (rhi.no'phi.lum. Gr. fem. n. rhis, nose; Gr. masc. adj. philos, loving; N.L. neut. adj. rhinophilum, nose-loving, referring to the site from which the strains were isolated).
Cells are Gram-positive, catalase-positive, oxidase-negative, non-spore-forming, non-motile bacilli (1.21.7m long and 0.70.9m wide). Optimal growth on solid medium was observed on BHI agar with 1% Tween 80 with incubation aerobically at 37C in a 5% CO2-enriched environment. Colonies on this medium measure 24mm and appear creamy white, exhibiting a raised or umbonate surface. Optimal growth in liquid medium was observed in BHI broth with 0.2% Tween 80; growth in TSB with 0.2% Tween 80 was also observed. In biochemical testing, strains were found to have alkaline phosphatase activity and showed variable pyrrolidonyl arylamidase activity and nitrate reduction. Fermentation of d-glucose and d-ribose was observed in a single strain. The major fatty acids identified were oleic (C18:19c) and palmitic (C16:0) acids. The genome of strain MSK072 has a size of 2.57 Mbp and a G+C content of 58.8mol%.
The type strain, MSK072T (=DSM 118652T=JCM 37534T), was isolated from a nasal swab collected in October 2019 from a 39-year-old male adult in Durham, NC, USA (30.01401 N, 78.94002 W). The partial 16S rRNA gene sequence and complete genome sequence of strain MSK072T have been deposited in GenBank under accession numbers PQ450554 and GCA_030232185.2, respectively.
